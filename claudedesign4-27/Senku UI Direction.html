Senku Android — UI direction

Today's UI works. It survives every test in the screenshot suite, the offline pack ships, the deterministic and generative answer paths both land on a usable detail screen. The problem is not behavior — it's hierarchy. Guide reader and answer detail wear the same uniform; meta pills stack three deep; the toolbar fights the content for breathing room; tablet landscape is a phone with sidebars bolted on. This document proposes a quieter, more purpose-built direction that still feels like Senku and lands incrementally on the existing Java/XML codebase.

1 · Critique of the current UI

1.1 Two surfaces, one costume

The biggest legibility issue is that guide pages and answer pages look identical. Both run on the dark olive canvas, both use the same parchment card with a left rule, both open with a FIELD HEADER · Field manual stamp. A user landing mid-task can't tell whether they are reading source or generated guidance — and that distinction is the whole trust model. DetailGuideContextPresentationFormatter and DetailAnswerPresentationFormatter currently render into roughly the same shell.

1.2 Pill stacks and chrome inflation

The toolbar capsule (bg_detail_topbar_group) groups back / home / pin / share into a floating chip cluster. It then sits next to a title, then below it a header pill (bg_detail_header_baseline), then below that a hero pill (bg_hero_panel) carrying the question. On a generative answer screen we count up to eight mono-caps chips on a single row: ANSWERED · THIS DEVICE · 3 SOURCES · 1 TURN · UNSURE FIT · REV 04-27 · PK 2 · #BCA1DC3D. Each is meaningful, but eight equally-weighted pieces of metadata read as visual static.

Every container has a stroke, a corner radius, and a faint inner gradient — so nothing contains anything; everything just floats next to everything else.

1.3 Phone landscape is portrait turned sideways

Screenshot home_entry in landscape preserves the same vertical search shell, the same stacked Browse/Ask segmented control, and pushes "Recent threads" off the right of the viewport into a half-visible second column. The status strip and identity row eat ~120px of vertical space, leaving only 280px of content height — there is no actual landscape-native layout.

1.4 Tablet landscape is phone-with-rails

The 3-pane tablet shot (guide_cross_reference_tablet_landscape) is structurally correct — left thread / center guide / right evidence — but visually all three panes share the same olive surface, the same stroke, the same parchment card treatment. The eye treats it as one busy surface, not three coordinated regions. There is no surface ladder.

1.5 Brown body on dark olive

The parchment-card body uses warm-brown ink on a saturated olive background in the field-manual surface. WCAG contrast is borderline (around 4.2:1) and in direct sunlight — the realistic use case for a field-ready app — it falls below readable. The rev03 tokens already define paper_ink #1F2318 on paper #E9E1CF at 13.6:1; that's the right pair.

1.6 Composer floats inside the same chrome it sits above

bg_detail_followup_active uses the same panel grammar as the answer card. There is no surface separation between "this is content" and "this is where I type." The follow-up shell ends up reading as another piece of answer card.

2 · Recommended design direction

2.1 Two surfaces, two textures

The single most useful move in this redesign: guide reader and answer detail get different surface treatments. Same content density, same typography, different paper.

Guide reader

Parchment card on the dark canvas. Serif body in dark ink. Section labels in mono caps with a small leading rule. The page is a printed object you are reading.

Answer detail

No paper. Content lives directly on the dark canvas, with serif body in light ink. The question is the title. The answer is the body. Evidence is a separated rail or panel below.

This costs almost nothing to implement — the parchment drawable already exists (bg_chip_parchment, senku_card family). What changes is when it's used: guide content surfaces, never answer surfaces. Answer screens stop trying to be paper.

2.2 Identity strip replaces the toolbar capsule

Replace the floating bg_detail_topbar_group capsule with a flat 36dp identity strip: chevron-back, then a context tag in the new accent (GUIDE, ANSWER, THREAD, SEARCH) in JetBrains Mono caps, then the GD-id, then the truncatable title, then 2–3 icon-only actions on the right. Hairline rule below. No capsule, no inner gradient, no nested radii.

The win is horizontal: the strip is one row tall, doesn't compete with the title, and the context tag does the surface-distinction work for free — GUIDE in olive, ANSWER in sand, THREAD in moss. The dimens token senku_rev03_toolbar_button = 34dp already supports this; we just stop wrapping them.

2.3 Meta line, not meta wall

Collapse the chip wall into a single tab-stop separated mono line: GD-345 · 3 src · rev 04-27 · pk 2 · unsure. Confidence and danger states are the only ones that get a leading dot color; the rest are weight-equal. Long lines truncate with an inline more › that opens a provenance sheet (DetailProvenancePresentationFormatter) in full.

2.4 Surface ladder for tablet

Three panes, three shades. bg0 for the left rail (deepest), bg2 or paper for the center (the focused content), bg1 for the right evidence rail. A 1px hairline between, no double strokes, no shadow. The eye follows lightness from left navigation outward to the work surface.

2.5 Type

Lock in the rev03 type system. Inter Tight for UI and titles (-0.005 to -0.02 letter-spacing), Source Serif 4 for answer body and guide body (the long-read surface), JetBrains Mono at 11sp for caps metadata only. Three roles, three faces — anything else (system sans, AppCompat caption) gets phased out.

2.6 Cards, panels, buttons, chips

  • Card containment rule: a region is either a paper card (parchment, dark ink, drop shadow) or a canvas region (no fill, hairline border, light ink). Never both. Kill the inner-gradient layer in bg_chat_answer.xml / bg_evidence_panel.xml — the gradient adds nothing readable.
  • Buttons: primary = solid sand on dark; secondary = hairline-stroked ghost; destructive = solid copper. Drop the parchment-on-olive button variant — it reads as a chip.
  • Chips: two flavors only. Outline chip for filters and tags; solid chip only for status confidence. Today there are nine bg_chip_* drawables (fire, hybrid, lexical, medicine, olive, parchment, shelter, vector, water) — keep them as semantic color tokens, not as nine different chip shapes.
  • Two radii only: 10dp for inline chrome, 14dp for cards. The current mix of 17/18/21/22dp inside a single layer-list is the noise source.

2.7 Navigation

Phone portrait: bottom nav, three items (Library, Ask, Saved) — same as today, but with a 2px accent underline on the active item instead of a tinted pill. Phone landscape and tablet (both orientations): a 56–80dp side rail on the left edge with the brand mark on top, the same three items below. The current landscape behavior of keeping bottom nav at the bottom + putting nav-icon-rail on the left is a contradiction — pick one per orientation.

3 · Form-factor recommendations

3.1 Phone portrait — 360–412 dp wide

Single column. Identity strip, content, bottom nav. Composer docks at the bottom only on answer / thread surfaces. Provenance and related guides are collapsed sections below the answer body, not a side rail — there is no horizontal room and stacking is fine in this orientation.

3.2 Phone landscape — 712–880 dp wide × 360–412 dp tall

Switch to a 56dp left rail (icon-only) and split the body into two flexible columns when the surface supports it: answer + evidence side-by-side, guide TOC + body, search results + preview. Bottom nav goes away in landscape — the rail replaces it. Identity strip stays at 36dp; status bar gets reduced left/right padding.

The hard rule: vertical real estate is the scarce resource in landscape. Anything that can be made horizontal should be. Confidence + meta should be one row, not two. The composer should be a single line, not a multi-line panel.

3.3 Tablet portrait — 800–960 dp wide × 1240+ tall

80dp side rail with labels on the left. Two-pane center. For guide reader: TOC (220dp) + paper card. For answer detail: answer body (1.5fr) + evidence rail (1fr). Composer docks below the answer pane only — never below the evidence rail. The whole content region uses 28–32dp horizontal padding; this is where the parchment card can finally breathe with proper margins.

3.4 Tablet landscape — 1240+ × 880 dp

The marquee tablet posture. Three panes:

  • Rail (220dp): per-surface index — for an answer, the thread turns and the source list; for a guide, the TOC and section anchors. This is where the DetailThreadHistoryRenderer and DetailRelatedGuidePresentationFormatter naturally live.
  • Center (flex): the work surface. Paper for guide, canvas for answer. Composer docks here.
  • Evidence (320dp): source cards + cross-references, always-on but collapsible to a 44dp gutter.

Each pane gets its own bg shade (bg0/bg1+paper/bg1) so the hierarchy reads without explicit borders. Hairlines do the rest.

4 · Guide reader vs answer detail — the distinction

Guide reader is reference

  • Parchment card with drop shadow
  • Section-numbered (§1, §2…) with TOC rail
  • Header tag in FIELD MANUAL · REV 04-27
  • Source-anchored danger callouts (paper-tone paper_danger)
  • Related guides surface as required reading blocks inline
  • No composer; it's a book, not a chat

Answer detail is response

  • Direct on canvas, no paper card
  • Question is the page title; answer is the body
  • Header tag in ANSWER · THIS DEVICE · UNSURE
  • Confidence is a single dot+word, not a chip-cluster
  • Evidence is a structured rail (id / role / quote / match%)
  • Composer is always present, anchored to the bottom

5 · Evidence and cross-reference

The current evidence rail has three problems: it's full-width even on tablet, the source cards have full chrome (border + gradient + parchment fill + left rule = four containers), and the relationship between an answer claim and a source quote is not visually traceable.

Recommended evidence card

Six elements in fixed order: id (mono, accent) · role (anchor / related / required, mono caps) · match% (right-aligned, mono) on one row; title on row 2 in UI semibold; quote on row 3 in italic serif at small body size; optional anchor section on row 4. Background is bg2, only decoration is a 2dp left rule in accent for anchor / olive60 for related / warn for required. No outer card border, just a hairline above and below.

Cross-reference (related guides)

On phone, list rows below the answer: id + title + chevron, separated by hairlines. On tablet, the right rail. Cross-reference roles get colored 2dp left rules to match evidence semantics — same vocabulary across the app.

6 · Follow-up composer

Recommendation: persistent docked at the bottom on answer/thread surfaces only. Never on guide reader. Ghost-styled (no card chrome, just a hairline rule above), single-line placeholder, with a context hint above the input (GD-345 · context kept · 3 sources visible). The hint is the trust signal: the user knows what scope their next question will inherit.

Send button: ghost-stroke until the user types, then solid sand. No FAB. No inline-at-end behavior — that adds a state machine for marginal benefit and complicates the DetailFollowupActive background drawable.

7 · Emergency / safety states

Stay calm, never alarmist. The current bg_emergency_banner is too red and too rounded — it reads like a marketing alert. Use:

  • Top rule, not banner. A 2dp solid danger stroke at the top of the surface, with a tinted region (8% danger over canvas) below it.
  • Numbered actions, not paragraphs. Steps as 22dp numbered circles; action verb in body, sub-line in muted.
  • Single source anchor. One evidence card, prominently. Emergency responses with multiple sources erode confidence in context — pick the canonical guide and link.
  • No animation. No icon. No exclamation marks. The word DANGER in mono caps with a single dot does the work.

9 · Risks & tradeoffs

  • Two surfaces, two costumes sounds elegant but doubles the surface treatments engineers have to keep consistent. Mitigate by enforcing the two-surface rule in DetailSurfaceContract with a single enum, not free-text.
  • Side rail on phone landscape deviates from Material defaults; some users may expect a top app bar. The win in vertical room is large enough that this is the right call, but it does mean writing landscape XML once and maintaining it.
  • Removing the chip wall hides metadata behind a "more" affordance. Power users (and the integration tests) currently depend on seeing every pill at once. Solution: keep the data accessible via the provenance sheet, not the front-page row.
  • Calm emergency state is an explicit choice. If product wants attention-grabbing, this direction will feel underweight. Hold the line — danger copy is loud enough; the chrome shouldn't shout.
  • Source-quote in evidence cards requires the runtime to surface a canonical excerpt per source (AnswerCardClause already carries a quoted_span; just thread it through DetailSourcePresentationFormatter).
  • Tablet 3-pane demands real responsive thinking — what collapses first on a smaller tablet, what evidence is "always on" vs "on demand". Punting on this means tablet is permanently wide-phone.
