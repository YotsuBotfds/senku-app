## Source Seeds

### Layperson Stroke Recognition

Primary source:
- CDC, [`Signs and Symptoms of Stroke`](https://www.cdc.gov/stroke/signs-symptoms/index.html) (Oct. 24, 2024)

Why it matters:
- if the corpus later gets a stroke-recognition expansion, the layperson framing should stay simple and urgent
- the main usable structure is `F.A.S.T.` plus immediate emergency action

Key verification points to preserve:
- sudden face droop / arm weakness / speech trouble
- every minute counts
- call emergency services immediately
- note the time symptoms started

### Layperson Heart Attack Warning Signs

Primary source:
- NHLBI, [`Heart Attack Wallet Card: Know the warning signs`](https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/resources/heart-attack-wallet-card-know-warning-signs) (Nov. 2020)
- NHLBI, [`Heart Attack - Symptoms`](https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/heart-attack/symptoms)

Why it matters:
- if the current emergency / cardiac guidance later needs a layperson-first expansion, it should stay anchored in plain warning-sign language rather than clinical theory

Key verification points to preserve:
- chest discomfort / pressure
- discomfort in arm, back, neck, jaw, shoulder, or upper stomach
- shortness of breath
- light-headedness, cold sweat, nausea
- call emergency services immediately

### Dementia / Older-Adult Home Safety

Primary source:
- NIA, [`Worksheet: Home Safety Checklist`](https://www.nia.nih.gov/sites/default/files/2023-04/worksheet-home-safety-checklist_1.pdf) (2023 PDF)

Why it matters:
- there is already an elder-dementia guide, but if another pass expands home-safety or accessibility content, it should stay grounded in concrete hazard-reduction steps

Key verification points to preserve:
- lighting
- trip hazards
- railings / ramps / gates where needed
- smoke and carbon monoxide alarms
- medication labeling and storage
- secure storage for firearms and other hazards

## Not Ready For Drafting By Default

These sources are **research seeds**, not permission to open a new guide immediately.

Before drafting:
1. finish current prompt-pack validation
2. confirm the topic is still a real retrieval or content miss
3. decide whether the fix is:
   - a focused guide
   - a layperson-facing section added to an existing guide
   - or just better routing into material that is already present
