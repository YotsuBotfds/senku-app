## Dentistry router

Use this page only as the intake and routing layer. Start by classifying the
concern, logging what is observable, screening red flags, and moving the user to
the focused owner guide or to a dentist, clinician, or emergency pathway.

- Routine brushing, flossing, rinsing, mild non-escalating gum irritation, and
  prevention -> [preventive-dental-hygiene](./preventive-dental-hygiene.md)
- Swelling, pus, fever, severe or worsening pain, uncontrolled bleeding,
  tooth looseness or displacement, broken tooth trauma, trouble swallowing, or
  trouble breathing -> [emergency-dental](./emergency-dental.md)
- Broken filling, loose crown, cavity concern, tooth fracture, restoration
  discomfort, or post-restoration sensitivity -> [dentistry-restorative](./dentistry-restorative.md)
- Dentures, partials, retainers, mouthguards, fit, rubbing, looseness, cleaning,
  or prosthetic comfort concerns -> [dental-prosthetics](./dental-prosthetics.md)
- Braces, aligners, brackets, wires, elastics, orthodontic rubbing, poking,
  looseness, or missing parts -> [orthodontics-survival](./orthodontics-survival.md)
- Tooth, jaw, gum, palate, salivary-gland, TMJ, and mouth-area terminology ->
  [anatomy-oral](./anatomy-oral.md)

## Reviewed Answer-Card Boundary

This is the reviewed answer-card surface for GD-351. Use it for dental concern
intake only: classify the concern as pain, swelling, trauma, hygiene,
restoration, prosthetic, orthodontic, or anatomy; record location and timing;
screen red flags; and route to the specific dental owner card, dentist,
clinician, emergency dental care, emergency department, EMS, or local emergency
pathway.

Start with observable details: upper or lower, right or left, front or back,
tooth or gum area, cheek side or tongue side, jaw, face, palate, tongue, lip,
under-jaw area, or appliance type if present. Log when it started, what changed,
pain pattern, swelling or drainage, fever, trauma, bleeding, ability to swallow
and breathe, chewing or biting triggers, restoration or appliance status, and
whether symptoms are stable, improving, or worsening.

Red flags leave this router immediately: trouble breathing, trouble swallowing,
drooling, voice change, tongue or floor-of-mouth swelling, spreading facial,
jaw, neck, gum, or near-eye swelling, fever with dental pain, pus, foul drainage
or taste from one area, uncontrolled bleeding, major trauma, knocked-out,
displaced, or very loose tooth, bite change after injury, numbness after injury,
severe constant worsening throbbing or night-waking pain, or a possible
swallowed or inhaled appliance part.

Do not use this reviewed card for diagnosis, extraction, drilling, filling,
filing, incision, drainage, splinting, reimplantation, anesthetic or procedure
steps, antibiotics, pain-medication dosing, appliance fabrication, repair,
adjustment, return-to-chewing or work clearance, legal claims, or safety
certification.
