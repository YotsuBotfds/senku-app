## Overview

Most mosquito bites, gnat bites, flea bites, and mild bee or wasp stings are annoying but not dangerous. The usual problems are **itching, redness, local swelling, and repeated scratching**. Those symptoms are uncomfortable, but they are often best handled with simple home care:

- Clean the skin
- Cool the area
- Reduce swelling
- Block scratching
- Watch for infection or an allergic reaction

This guide is written for direct questions like:

- "How do I stop mosquito bites from itching?"
- "This sting is swollen. What should I do?"
- "How do I tell if a bug bite is serious?"

The short answer is: **cool it, clean it, protect it, and watch it.**

## First Steps

### What to do right away

1. Wash your hands if you can.
2. Wash the bite or sting with soap and clean water.
3. Remove any visible stinger or foreign material.
4. Apply a cold pack or cool wet cloth for 10 to 15 minutes.
5. Avoid scratching, squeezing, or puncturing the skin.

### What not to do

- Do not scratch it open.
- Do not pop blisters.
- Do not apply heat to an itchy bite.
- Do not use bleach, alcohol, or harsh chemicals on the skin.
- Do not assume every red, swollen bite is harmless if it keeps getting worse.

## How Do I Stop Mosquito Bites from Itching?

### Best first-line relief for itching

**Cold is usually the fastest help.**

- Ice pack wrapped in cloth
- Cool wet washcloth
- Cool shower or bath

Use 10 to 15 minutes at a time, then repeat as needed. Cooling reduces itch by numbing skin nerves and slowing the inflammatory response.

### Simple itch-relief options

| Option | Best use | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cold compress | First-line for most bites | Safe, cheap, works fast |
| 1% hydrocortisone cream | Itchy red bites | Apply thinly; follow package directions |
| Calamine lotion | Dry, itchy skin | Helpful if skin is not broken |
| Oral antihistamine | Lots of bites or sleep-disrupting itch | Follow label directions; can cause drowsiness |
| Colloidal oatmeal bath | Multiple itchy bites | Good for widespread irritation |
| Baking soda paste | Small itchy spots | Use briefly, then rinse; stop if skin stings |

### When itch is the main problem

If the bite is mostly itchy and not infected:

- Keep it clean
- Keep it cool
- Use a thin layer of hydrocortisone or calamine
- Cover it if you keep absentmindedly scratching it

If itching is keeping you awake, a bedtime antihistamine may help, but follow the product label and avoid anything that is unsafe for children, pregnancy, or other health conditions.

## This Sting Is Swollen. What Should I Do?

### For local swelling after a bite or sting

Mild swelling is common, especially after:

- Mosquito bites
- Bee or wasp stings
- Biting flies
- Fire ant stings

Helpful care:

- Elevate the area if possible
- Use a cold compress
- Rest the limb if the bite is on an arm or leg
- Remove rings or tight jewelry if a hand or finger is swelling

### What swelling is still normal

Swelling can be normal if it:

- Stays near the bite or sting
- Peaks within the first day
- Slowly improves over 1 to 3 days

### When swelling is more concerning

Seek care sooner if swelling:

- Spreads rapidly
- Becomes very painful instead of just itchy
- Involves the whole limb
- Causes numbness, tightness, or reduced movement
- Happens with hives, lip swelling, wheezing, or dizziness

## Cleaning the Bite or Sting

### Basic cleaning

For most bites and stings:

1. Wash with soap and clean running water.
2. Gently rinse off dirt, saliva, or plant debris.
3. Pat dry with a clean towel.
4. If skin is open, cover with a clean bandage.

### If the skin is broken

Broken skin needs a little more care:

- Keep it clean once or twice daily
- Use a plain clean bandage if friction will irritate it
- Replace wet or dirty dressings
- Watch for increasing redness, warmth, or drainage

### If there is a bee sting

If a honeybee stinger is still in the skin, remove it as soon as possible. Scrape it out if you can. Do not spend a long time digging at it; quick removal matters more than perfect technique.

### If there is a tick attached

Ticks are a little different from ordinary bites because they can spread disease.

- Remove the tick with fine-tipped tweezers if possible
- Pull straight out with steady pressure
- Clean the bite site afterward
- Watch for rash, fever, headache, or expanding redness in the following days

If you cannot remove a tick cleanly, or if you develop a target-shaped rash or flu-like illness afterward, get medical advice.

## Prevent Scratching

### Why scratching makes things worse

Scratching:

- Breaks the skin
- Raises infection risk
- Makes swelling last longer
- Can turn a small bite into a bigger wound

### Ways to stop scratching

- Cover the bite with a bandage or light wrap
- Trim fingernails short
- Put a cold cloth on it when the urge to scratch starts
- Use lotion or cream before the skin dries out
- Distract the hands, especially at night

For children, covering the bite and keeping nails short is often the most useful combination.

## Home Comfort Measures

### Low-effort comfort measures that help most people

- Cool bath or shower
- Loose clothing over the area
- Rest and elevation
- Hydration if you are warm or sweaty
- Sleep support if itching is keeping you awake

### When a bath helps

An oatmeal bath or cool soak can help if there are many itchy bites. This is useful for mosquito-heavy evenings, flea exposure, or clusters of small bites.

### When a wrap helps

A light bandage can help if:

- You keep scratching while asleep
- Clothing rubs the spot
- The bite is on a joint or high-friction area

Do not wrap tightly. Tight wraps can worsen swelling.

### Helpful household supplies

- Soap and clean water
- Cold pack or ice wrapped in cloth
- Hydrocortisone cream
- Calamine lotion
- Bandages or gauze
- Oral antihistamine per label directions

## Common Bites and Stings

### Mosquito bites

Mosquito bites usually cause small itchy bumps with a central spot. They are uncomfortable but usually not dangerous.

Best care:

- Cold compress
- Anti-itch lotion or hydrocortisone
- Prevent scratching

### Flea bites

Flea bites often cluster around ankles or lower legs. Itching can be intense.

Best care:

- Wash the skin
- Use cold compresses
- Treat the source of bites if they keep happening

### Bed bug bites

Bed bug bites may appear in lines or clusters. They often show up after sleep and may itch a lot.

Best care:

- Cool the skin
- Prevent scratching
- Check bedding and sleeping areas if bites keep appearing

### Bee and wasp stings

These often hurt right away, then swell and itch later.

Best care:

- Remove stinger if present
- Cold compress
- Rest and elevate the area
- Watch for allergy signs

### Fire ant stings

Fire ant stings can burn, itch, and form small pustules later.

Best care:

- Clean the area
- Cold compress
- Do not pop pustules
- Watch for multiple stings or allergic symptoms

### Spider bites

Most suspected spider bites are minor skin irritations or other bites mistaken for spiders. A small itchy red bump is usually not serious.

Get help sooner if the bite becomes:

- Very painful
- Rapidly enlarging
- Blistering or blackening
- Associated with muscle cramping, sweating, nausea, or fever

## How Do I Tell If a Bug Bite Is Serious?

### Infection warning signs

Watch for signs that a bite is turning into a skin infection:

- Redness that keeps spreading
- Increasing warmth
- Pus or cloudy drainage
- Worsening pain after the first day
- Red streaks moving away from the bite
- Fever
- Tender swollen lymph nodes

### Dangerous allergic reaction warning signs

Seek emergency help if the person develops:

- Trouble breathing
- Wheezing or noisy breathing
- Swelling of the lips, tongue, throat, or face
- Widespread hives
- Fainting or severe dizziness
- Repeated vomiting
- A feeling that "something is very wrong" after a sting

### Other red flags

Get medical evaluation if:

- The swelling is severe or rapidly expanding
- The person has diabetes, immune suppression, or poor circulation
- The bite is on the eye, mouth, or genitals
- A child becomes lethargic or unusually irritable
- The wound turns black, purple, or blistered
- The person has fever or body aches after tick exposure

## A Simple Decision Rule

If the bite or sting is:

- **Itchy only** -> cool it, treat the itch, and avoid scratching
- **Swollen but stable** -> elevate it and use cold packs
- **Broken and red** -> clean it and monitor for infection
- **With breathing trouble or face swelling** -> emergency care now
- **Getting worse day by day** -> get medical evaluation

## Quick Answers

### How do I stop mosquito bites from itching?

Wash the area, apply a cold compress, then use calamine or 1% hydrocortisone if the skin is intact. Cover it if you keep scratching.

### This sting is swollen. What should I do?

Use cold compresses, elevate the area, remove tight jewelry, and watch whether swelling stays local or starts spreading. If the swelling is severe, rapidly worsening, or affects breathing or the face, get emergency help.

### How do I tell if a bug bite is serious?

A bite is more serious if it has spreading redness, pus, fever, red streaks, severe pain, black skin, facial swelling, or any trouble breathing.

## Prevention

The best treatment is avoiding repeated bites:

- Wear long sleeves and pants in high-insect areas
- Use insect repellent as directed
- Remove standing water near living spaces
- Sleep under netting if mosquitoes are common
- Check skin and bedding after exposure

Repeated bites often mean the environment needs attention, not just the skin.
