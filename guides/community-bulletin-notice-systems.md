## Overview: Information Distribution at Community Scale

In low-infrastructure environments, public notices distributed through bulletin boards and town criers remain the backbone of community communication. This guide covers designing bulletin systems that reach diverse audiences, training criers for consistency, and establishing protocols that balance public information access with message integrity.

## Physical Bulletin Board Design and Construction

### Board Specifications

**Standard community bulletin board:**

<div class="board-spec">
**Size:** 1.2 m wide Ã— 1.5 m tall (large enough to hold ~50 notices, visible from 3 meters away)
**Material:** Wood (pine or oak frame, cork or wood panel backing)
**Surface:** Cork board or soft wood (allows tacking; gentle on paper)
**Backing:** Painted frame with weather-sealed exterior (paint repels water, extends board life)
**Posts:** Sturdy wooden posts (10 cm Ã— 10 cm), sunk 0.5 m into ground or concrete
**Height:** Bottom edge at 1 meter height (accessible to standing adults, children can reach with effort)
</div>

**Construction sequence:**

1. **Frame:** Construct wooden frame (1.2 m wide Ã— 1.5 m tall) from 5 cm Ã— 10 cm lumber
2. **Backing:** Attach cork board or soft-wood panel to frame
3. **Protective border:** Wooden trim (2 cm Ã— 5 cm) on all sides (protects edges, improves appearance)
4. **Finish:** Paint frame and trim weatherproof paint (two coats); leave cork/backing natural or seal lightly
5. **Mounting:** Attach to posts at 1 meter height; ensure stable (wind resistance)
6. **Roof (optional):** Angled roof overhang (30 cm Ã— 1.3 m) protects notices from rain; improves visibility

**Cost estimate (simple design):**
- Materials: 40â€“80 shillings (lumber, cork, nails, paint)
- Labor: 16â€“24 hours (can be community work day)
- Lifespan: 5â€“10 years with maintenance

### Board Location and Placement

**Ideal locations (priority ranking):**

1. **Central marketplace:** Busiest location, all community passes through
2. **Community meeting hall:** Regular gathering point
3. **Main road junction:** Travelers and locals cross multiple times weekly
4. **Market area:** Where commerce occurs (people look for price updates, trade info)

**Secondary locations (if community large or geographically dispersed):**

5. Town square/gathering area
6. Water source (well, stream) where people gather daily
7. Major workplace (mill, forge, farm collective)
8. Religious gathering place (temple, church)

**Placement considerations:**

- **Visibility:** Position where people naturally look (don't hide behind structures)
- **Lighting:** Morning/afternoon sun helps readability; avoid deep shadows
- **Weather protection:** Some roof overhang; not in direct rainfall path
- **Safety:** Position away from doorways or traffic paths (people stop to read, shouldn't block passage)
- **Security:** Visible from community leaders' offices (discourages tampering)

### Board Maintenance

**Daily:**
- Check for water damage; remove wet notices
- Verify pinning is secure (wind may loosen notices)
- Note if critical notices are missing or obscured

**Weekly:**
- Remove expired notices (more than 3 weeks old or past deadline date)
- Organize remaining notices (group by category)
- Clean dirt/mud from board surface

**Monthly:**
- Paint touch-ups if frame is chipped
- Verify cork/backing surface is in good condition (replace if degraded)
- Check structural stability (ensure posts aren't rotting, frame is plumb)

**Quarterly:**
- Full-board repaint or refinish if needed
- Replace damaged cork or backing panel
- Structural inspection (roof integrity, post condition)

:::tip
Appoint a "board keeper" (paid or volunteer) responsible for daily maintenance. Neglected boards become cluttered and unreadable, reducing their effectiveness.
:::

### Multi-Board Systems (Large Communities)

For communities with 500+ people or spread over 5+ km:

**Establish 3â€“4 boards:**
- Primary board (marketplace): All critical notices
- Secondary board (meeting hall): Meeting times, governance
- Tertiary board (outlying area): Local announcements for that district
- Duplicate critical notices across all boards (ensures reach)

**Board coordination:**
- All boards updated same day by scribe or designated person
- Standardized content: Critical notices identical on all boards
- Different boards may have locally relevant additions

## Standardized Notice Format

Consistent format makes notices scannable and accessible.

### Text Notice Template

```
=====================================
NOTICE TO COMMUNITY
=====================================

SUBJECT: [One-line title]
DATE POSTED: [Day, Date, Time]
EFFECTIVE DATE: [When this takes effect]
EXPIRATION DATE: [When this no longer applies]

AUTHORITY: [Who is announcing - Mayor, Council, Market Master, etc.]

CONTENT:
[Main message, 2-3 paragraphs maximum]

ACTION REQUIRED: [What people should do, if anything]
- [Specific action 1]
- [Specific action 2]

LOCATION: [Where this applies or where to go]
TIME: [When this happens or deadline]
CONTACT: [Who to ask questions - name and location]

Authorized by: _____________ [Signature]
Posted by: _____________ [Board keeper name/mark]
```

### Notice Categories and Color-Coding

Use colored paper to enable quick scanning:

| Category | Color | Content |
|---|---|---|
| EMERGENCY | Red | Immediate danger, evacuation, security threat |
| GOVERNANCE | Blue | Laws, council decisions, meeting times |
| MARKET/TRADE | Green | Market dates, prices, trading information |
| COMMUNITY | Yellow | Social events, festivals, volunteers needed |
| PUBLIC WORKS | Orange | Road repair, building projects, work schedules |
| MEETING | White | Town meetings, public forums |

**Implementation:**
- Print text on colored paper (or use colored background banner at top)
- Color immediately signals urgency and category
- Literate people can quickly find relevant notices; illiterate people can ask "Is this the red (emergency) board?"

### Character and Language Standards

**Legible writing:**
- Large, clear handwriting (2 cm letter height minimum)
- Standard ink (black or dark blue, legible)
- Typed or carefully printed (avoid cursive if low literacy common)
- Consistent: Same hand/machine for all notices improves recognition

**Language:**
- Simple, direct wording (avoid jargon)
- Short sentences
- Active voice: "The market opens Tuesday" not "It is expected that opening will occur"
- No abbreviations (spell out dates, names, locations fully)

## Town Crier Protocols and Announcement Procedures

For public announcements reaching those who don't read boards (working in fields, traveling, illiterate).

### Crier Role and Selection

**Town crier responsibilities:**
- Memorize key announcements for the week
- Ring bell at designated hours to gather attention
- Read/recite announcements clearly and loudly
- Repeat announcements (people miss first telling)
- Answer basic questions about notices

**Crier selection:**
- Good voice (loud, clear, not nasal or harsh)
- Reliable (shows up on schedule every day)
- Memory (can recite announcements accurately)
- Trusted (won't alter messages, won't gossip about content)
- Patience (answers repeated questions without frustration)

### Crier Schedule

**Standard calling schedule:**

- **Early morning (6â€“7 am):** Announce day's events for people starting work
- **Noon (12 pm):** Market hours, weather announcements, urgent updates
- **Evening (5â€“6 pm):** Gather workers returning from fields; announce next day's events

**Equipment:**
- Bell or hand-rung gong (carries 500 meters in calm weather)
- Written list of announcements (paper, carved on wood, or memorized)
- Formal attire (marks crier as official, not just random shouting)

### Announcement Scripting

**Standard crier format:**

<div class="crier-script">
RING BELL LOUDLY (3 times to gather attention)

"Hear ye, hear ye! It is [time] on [day]. The following announcements for [community name]:"

[ANNOUNCEMENT 1]
[ANNOUNCEMENT 2]
[ANNOUNCEMENT 3]

"These announcements are posted on the bulletin board at the marketplace. God preserve the community! Come ye all to the town meeting [day and time if applicable]!"

RING BELL TWICE (closing signal)
</div>

**Example announcement:**

<div class="crier-script">
RING BELL (3 times)

"Hear ye, hear ye! It is noon on Wednesday the 22nd of February, in the year 2026, in the community of [Name]."

"First: The market will be OPEN on Saturday from sunrise to noon. Bring your goods for trade. The Market Master will enforce honest weights."

"Second: EMERGENCY NOTICE: Water well in North District is CLOSED for repair. Use the South well until further notice. The repair will be completed by Thursday."

"Third: TOWN MEETING scheduled for Sunday evening at the community hall. All residents invited. We will discuss the road repair and the new tax assessment."

"These announcements are POSTED on the bulletin board. Questions? Ask the Town Clerk at the Hall."

RING BELL (2 times)
</div>

### Training the Crier

**Week 1â€“2: Observation**
- Crier shadows current crier (if one exists)
- Learns calling times, locations, and routines
- Learns how to ring bell loudly without hurting ears

**Week 3â€“4: Supervised practice**
- Crier reads announcements from board while supervisor listens
- Practices volume and clarity (must be heard from 100 meters)
- Learns to engage with questions (answer patiently, refer complex questions to authorities)

**Week 5+: Solo duty**
- Crier calls announcements independently
- Leadership observes occasionally to verify quality
- Monthly feedback (clear voice? Accurate announcements? On schedule?)

## Message Prioritization and Urgency Levels

Not all notices are equally urgent. Clear prioritization ensures critical information reaches people.

### Notice Priority Levels

<div class="notice-priority">

**LEVEL 1: EMERGENCY (Red notices)**
- Immediate danger (fire, flooding, military threat)
- Immediate action required (evacuate, take shelter)
- Posted immediately, announced every 2 hours by crier
- Must reach every person within 24 hours
- Examples: Approaching army, dam failure, plague outbreak

**LEVEL 2: URGENT (Orange notices)**
- Time-sensitive governance or safety issue
- Action required within days
- Posted immediately, announced at noon and evening by crier
- Reaches most people within 48 hours
- Examples: Public works emergency, disease warning, market date change

**LEVEL 3: IMPORTANT (Blue notices)**
- Governance or community matter of significance
- Action required within weeks
- Posted and announced once per day for first week
- Reaches most people within 1 week
- Examples: Law change, tax deadline, meeting notice

**LEVEL 4: ROUTINE (Green/Yellow notices)**
- Standard announcements (market hours, volunteer opportunities)
- No urgent action required
- Posted on bulletin board, announced 1â€“2 times per week
- Reaches people within 2 weeks
- Examples: Weekly market schedule, community event, volunteer request

</div>

## Emergency Alert System

For truly critical situations requiring immediate action (natural disaster, military threat, plague).

### Multi-Channel Alert Protocol

**Sequence of actions (simultaneous or rapid succession):**

1. **Bell ringing (immediate):** Continuous loud ringing (5+ minutes) = Emergency signal recognized by all
2. **Crier announcement (within 30 minutes):** Crier reads emergency notice from memory/paper
3. **Bulletin board posting (within 1 hour):** Emergency notice posted for those who missed announcement
4. **Relay to outlying areas (within 2 hours):** Runner/courier carries news to farms, workplaces, neighboring communities
5. **Repeated announcements (ongoing):** Crier repeats every hour until situation resolved

**Example: Flood Warning Cascade**

```
Time 0: Flood watch reported
Time 5 min: Bells ring continuously
Time 15 min: Crier reads: "FLOOD WARNING - Water level rising rapidly. Be ready to evacuate. Go to high ground if water enters your home."
Time 45 min: Notice posted on board with detailed location of safe areas
Time 90 min: Couriers take notices to outlying farms
Time 6 hr: Continue crier announcements every 1â€“2 hours
```

### Message Content for Emergency Alerts

Emergency notices must convey:
- **What happened:** Clear description
- **What to do:** Specific action (stay, evacuate, shelter-in-place, prepare)
- **Where to go:** Safe locations or assembly points
- **When:** Deadline for action
- **Who:** Leadership/authority for questions

## Record-Keeping and Notice Archives

Maintain permanent record of all posted notices for governance and reference.

### Notice Log

**Entry format:**

```
DATE POSTED: 22 Feb 2026
NOTICE ID: GOV-2026-047
CATEGORY: [Emergency/Governance/Market/Community/etc.]
PRIORITY: [1-4]
TITLE: [Brief title of notice]
CONTENT SUMMARY: [1-2 sentences]
POSTED BY: [Name of person posting]
ANNOUNCED BY CRIER: [Y/N] [When]
EXPIRATION DATE: [When notice should be removed]
REMOVAL DATE: [Actual date removed]
NOTES: [Any relevant details - weather damage, tampering, feedback]
```

**Archive storage:**
- Monthly log sheets compiled into yearly ledger
- Stored at Town Hall or community record repository
- Protected from moisture (sealed container, waterproof storage area)
- Accessible for public inspection (governance transparency)

### Feedback and Comments Log

Allow community to provide feedback on notices and communication:

**Feedback form (posted next to bulletin board):**

```
COMMUNICATION FEEDBACK

What notice was unclear?
[Space for response]

Did you understand the announcement?
[Yes / No / Partially]

Was the information helpful?
[Yes / No / Why not?]

Suggestions for improvement:
[Space for response]

Optional: Your name: _______________
```

**Review process:**
- Board keeper collects feedback weekly
- Reviews with governance leadership monthly
- Adjust notice format if multiple people misunderstood
- Share high-quality feedback with crier (helps improve announcements)

:::tip
Community feedback improves notice effectiveness over time. Don't see it as criticism but as help improving communication.

## Supporting Low-Literacy Communities

Many people may not readâ€”ensure they still access critical information.

### Community Readers Program

Designated literate community members assist in reading:

**Recruitment:**
- Ask for volunteers (schoolteacher, clerk, literate elder)
- Offer small stipend or tax relief
- Clarify that role is reading to others, not interpreting (reader reports exactly what notice says)

**Reader duties:**
- Station at bulletin board 2â€“3 hours per day (morning and evening)
- Read notices aloud to anyone who asks
- Answer basic questions ("What does this notice say?")
- Refer complex questions to authorities

**Reader training:**
- Learn to read slowly and clearly
- Practice explaining date/time concepts (some may not count numerals)
- Learn to be patient (people may ask same question multiple times)
- Memorize key symbols/icons (help illiterate people find relevant notices)

### Oral Tradition and Story-Telling

For cultures with strong oral traditions:

**Community storyteller:**
- Memorize key announcements (learned from reader)
- Retell at evening gatherings in entertaining way
- Embeds notices in familiar story format (easier to remember)
- Encouraged by governance (seen as important cultural role)

**Example storytelling approach:**

Instead of: "Market will be open Saturday at sunrise"

Tell as: "When the first light touches the eastern hill, the market drums will sound. Bring your best goodsâ€”traders from the Valley will come with fresh cloth and metal tools. The Market Master will ensure fair weights, as always..."

### Writing System Supports

For communities with limited literacy:

- Use **consistent visual language** (same symbols always mean same thing)
- **Color-code notices** (red = emergency, green = normal)
- **Pictures/drawings** of what's being announced (fire danger = draw fire)
- **Repetition through multiple channels** (bulletin board + crier + runner announcements)

## Integration with Community Governance

Bulletin systems are formal communication arm of governance.

### Authority and Approval

**Notice approval authority:**
- Mayor or council must approve all Level 1â€“2 (Emergency/Urgent) notices before posting
- Department heads may post Level 3â€“4 (Routine/Important) notices under delegation
- Any notice changing law or governance must be approved by council
- Public inspection right: Anyone can ask "Who approved this notice?" and receive answer

**Signature requirement:**
- All notices signed by authorizing official
- Signature visible on notice (builds trust, creates accountability)
- Forged notices are serious crime (protected by law)

### Public Record Status

Notices are public documents:
- Anyone can view posted notices (no restriction)
- Copies available from Town Hall (upon request)
- Archive accessible to researchers/citizens (governance transparency)
- Historical notices preserved for reference and learning

### Community Feedback to Governance

Notices can include mechanisms for citizen input:

**Example:**
```
PROPOSED LAW: New market tax

This proposed law will take effect 1 March 2026.
Read the full text posted at Town Hall.

COMMUNITY INPUT PERIOD: 15 Feb â€“ 22 Feb 2026

Attend Town Council on Friday 24 Feb to voice support or concerns.
Cannot attend? Write your thoughts, seal in envelope, leave at Town Hall.

This law will be decided by community vote on 28 Feb.
```

**Benefits:**
- Citizens know how to engage with governance
- Feedback informs final decision-making
- Builds legitimacy of decisions (people feel heard even if they disagree)
